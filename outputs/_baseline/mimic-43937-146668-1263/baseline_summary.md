## HPI
87 yo wheelchair-bound male with CAD s/p PCI, ischemic cardiomyopathy (EF 30%), prior CVA, HTN, dementia, and recurrent aspiration PNA, recently admitted for acute on chronic systolic CHF exacerbation, presenting from nursing home with respiratory distress, found unresponsive with hypoxia and hypotension.

## Active Problems
Respiratory distress requiring intubation with bibasilar rales and bibasilar infiltrates on CXR; sepsis with fever 102.8, leukocytosis, positive UA, purulent sputum, and hypotension requiring norepinephrine and vasopressin. Blood and sputum cultures grew oxacillin-sensitive Staph aureus (resulted after expiration). Cardiac: CAD s/p PCI of D1 ([**7-/2191**]), ischemic cardiomyopathy with EF 30%, acute on chronic systolic CHF with element of volume overload on CXR, elevated troponins with EKG showing wide-complex regular tachycardia at 140, LAD, biventricular block, ST elevation in lead III in context of LBBB — felt by Cardiology to likely represent demand ischemia from sepsis. Empirically started on heparin drip for possible PE. Renal/metabolic: acute kidney injury thought secondary to dehydration, hypernatremia (Na 150) attributed to poor free water intake, lactic acidosis (lactate up to 8.9). Coags uptrended, concerning for DIC, but fibrinogen normal. Sacral stage 2 decubitus ulcer. Foley draining yellow-beige pus. PMH: CVA ([**2187**]), HTN, dementia, recurrent aspiration PNA, depression, GERD, neurogenic bladder, gout, BPH. Allergies: Depakote, Zarontin, Phenobarbital, Aspirin. Patient was DNR/DNI; expired during admission.

## Plan

LENIs ordered but not completed. Echo recommended by Cardiology. Despite aggressive measures, patient became progressively hypotensive and bradycardic; per DNR/DNI status, no chest compressions performed. Patient expired. Guardian, wife, and son notified.
