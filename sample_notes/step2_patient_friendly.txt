# Your Hospital Stay Summary

## What Happened When You Arrived
*   **Found Down:** You were discovered lying on the floor at home. You had a history of Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE), which is an autoimmune disease, and obesity. You also have a history of thyroid lymphoma, which caused your thyroid to stop working properly (hypothyroidism).
*   **Heart Trouble:** When you arrived at the Emergency Department (ED), your heart was beating very slowly (bradycardia). It had stopped sending electrical signals correctly, a condition called **Complete Heart Block (CHB)**.
*   **Low Blood Pressure:** Your blood pressure was very low (hypotension).
*   **Emergency Care:** Your heart rate dropped to 20 beats per minute, which is dangerously slow. The doctors gave you emergency medicines (epinephrine, atropine, and glucagon) to try to speed up your heart.
*   **Transfer:** Because the medicines did not work, a temporary wire was placed through a vein in your neck to electrically pace your heart. You were put on a breathing machine (intubated) and given sedatives. You were then transferred to our hospital.

## Tests and Procedures Done
*   **Hard Collar:** You were wearing a stiff neck brace because you fell, but no one saw the fall happen.
*   **Head Scan (CT):** A CT scan of your head was done, but it showed no bleeding or injury.
*   **Heart Ultrasound (ECHO):** An ultrasound of your heart was done, but the results were not yet available.
*   **Temporary Pacemaker:** A wire was placed in your heart to help it beat regularly.
*   **Blood Tests:** Your blood magnesium and phosphate levels were low, so we gave you supplements to fix them.
*   **Artery Line:** A tube was placed in a large artery in your neck (right internal jugular vein) to measure your blood pressure and heart pressures accurately. This was done using X-ray guidance (fluoroscopy).
*   **Breathing Support:** You were on a ventilator (breathing machine) because your lungs were not working well on their own.

## Why These Tests and Procedures Were Important
*   **The Pacemaker:** Your heart was too slow to keep you alive. The temporary wire helped your heart beat at a normal speed (80–100 beats per minute) while we looked for the cause.
*   **The Artery Line:** Your blood pressure was very low and hard to control. We needed this line to get precise readings of your blood pressure and heart pressure so we could adjust your medicines correctly.
*   **The Ventilator:** Your oxygen levels were okay, but you had thick mucus in your lungs and were very sleepy. The machine helped you breathe while we treated your infection and waited for your heart to stabilize.

## Your Medical Problems (Diagnoses)
*   **Complete Heart Block:** Your heart's electrical system is broken, causing it to beat too slowly.
*   **Low Blood Pressure:** Your blood pressure is lower than normal, but not low enough to be called "shock."
*   **Fever:** You have a high temperature (up to 100.4°F / 38°C).
*   **Acute Respiratory Failure:** Your lungs are not working well enough to give your body enough oxygen on their own.
*   **Pain Control:** We are monitoring your pain and comfort levels.

## Medicines You Received
*   **Dopamine:** Started to raise your blood pressure. The dose was adjusted as you improved.
*   **Propofol:** A medicine to keep you asleep and calm.
*   **Midazolam:** Added to help you stay asleep and reduce the side effects of the dopamine.
*   **Antibiotics:** You were given three different antibiotics (Cefepime, Vancomycin, and Flagyl) to treat a suspected infection.
*   **Tylenol:** Given through a tube in your nose (NGT) to lower your fever, but it did not seem to work well because it was not absorbed properly.
*   **Cool Baths:** Used to help lower your body temperature.

## How You Responded to Treatment
*   **Heart:** The temporary pacemaker worked well. Your heart rate stayed between 80 and 100 beats per minute.
*   **Blood Pressure:** After giving you more fluids and adjusting your dopamine, your blood pressure improved to a safer range (90–100 systolic).
*   **Fever:** Your temperature stayed high (between 99.8°F and 100.4°F). The antibiotics are still being given to fight the infection.
*   **Breathing:** After placing the artery line, we were able to take you off the highest setting of the ventilator. Your oxygen levels remained above 94%.

## What Happens Next (Discharge Plan)
*   **Current Status:** You are still in the hospital.
*   **Waiting on Results:** We are waiting for final reports on your heart function, blood gases, and infection cultures.
*   **Follow-Up:** Your follow-up appointments are not yet scheduled.
*   **Going Home:** It is not yet documented when or if you will be going home.
*   **Warning Signs:** Specific instructions on when to call the doctor or return to the hospital were not written down.
