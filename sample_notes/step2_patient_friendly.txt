### Why You Came to the Hospital
You arrived at the hospital because you felt very weak. Your heart was beating too slowly, a condition called bradycardia (heart beating too slowly). Your blood pressure was also lower than normal. The doctors could not fix your heart rhythm with a temporary device.

### What We Found
Your heart's electrical signals had stopped working properly, known as complete heart block (your heart's signal stopped working). You had low blood pressure but were not in shock. You also had a high body temperature. Tests showed your lungs were failing because you aspirated food or liquid into them.

### What We Did
We placed a wire into your right side to help your heart beat. Doctors put a tube into your neck vein to measure blood pressure. You needed a breathing machine because you could not breathe well on your own. We gave you medicines to keep your heart and blood pressure stable.

### Your Medicines
We started giving you dopamine to raise your blood pressure. We also added propofol and midazolam to help you sleep. New antibiotics were started to treat possible infections in your blood. We will slowly reduce the amount of dopamine you receive over time.

### How You Are Doing
Your heart rhythm has returned to normal and is staying steady. We are still waiting on results for your heart echo test. Your urine output is increasing, which is a good sign. Your highest temperature today was 100.4 degrees Fahrenheit.

### What Happens Next
We are still investigating if your low blood pressure is from infection or heart issues. Your care team will continue to monitor your vital signs closely. You will likely stay in the hospital for more observation and treatment.
